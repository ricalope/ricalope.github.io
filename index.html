About

Ever since I can remember I was fascinated by seeing how things worked. Growing up I saw some of friends go off to become software developers, and now that I'm older I finally get to experience the beauty of creativity and exploration that programming brings.

I've spent the bulk of my career working in Customer Service, and since 2016 I've been working at PayPal in collections. Throughout my time working in these different industries I developed team building skills and ways to have difficult conversations effectively, while also doing my very best to empathize with the human on the other end of the line. During the pandemic I realized that I wanted something more for my life and it was in february of 2022 that my wife finally pushed me to chase my dream of being a full stack developer, so I quit my job at paypal and applied to App Academy where I was ultimately accepted.

To say I was unprepared for how difficult a full stack boot camp would be, is an understatement, it was an uphill climb but since I had just quit my job I committed to making this work no matter what. When I started out I acknowledged that I had little to no experience in this field and just asked as many questions as I could, took notes, and practiced until it finally clicked. The amount of help I received from instructors, as well as pair programming partners, was unbelievable, to think there's a wealth of knowledge out there and people were just giving it freely to those who would listen was inspiring. During my time at App Academy I spent a lot of time learning Javascript and the concepts around programming, and we were ultimately tasked with building 3 full stack applications, 2 of which were solo endeavors, but the 3rd, a group project, is where I really found my stride. We had a week to build out an app and we decided to build a clone of Stack Overflow called Snack Overflow where we utilized Python and Flask for the backend and React and Redux for the frontend and state management. Ultimately, even though all 4 of us had never worked together, we collaborated very well on the project and we all emphasized the need for communication so that way not any ONE person fell behind we all filled the cracks for each other and that culminated into us finishing the project 1 day early with 2 additional bonuses to get our project a score of 120%. Finishing that project was a life changing moment for me, not only seeing all the work I had put in at App Academy pay off, but also seeing the little things I had picked up through collections and customer service like teamwork, communication, and strategizing come to fruition as well.

Shortly after graduating AA I was having a hard time getting a job, which at the time, I assumed, was more due to my lack of experience than my technical knowledge. So after speaking to a cohortmate about this he sent me the details for FastTrack in the hopes that I might be able to get more experience from a place that works more directly with job seekers and knows a little more about what they're looking for. That leads me to here and now, I'm still currently enrolled in FastTrack and it's been a blast not only learning another language, Java, which is equal parts challenging and fun, but also learning what companies look for and getting help with telling my story. The first couple weeks in FastTrack were a blur between the ever looming deadline for a assignment i didn't see or the mock interviews that I usually forgot to prepare for. To cap the weeks of Java off I was tasked with developing a backend from a schema in our first group project using Java and Spring. My partner and I did a great job communicating and working through the endpoints and helping each other when needed, it was a tough backend with edge cases galore but ultimately we faced the challenge head on and both did our best to finish and pass. FastTrack always gave me enough information to do what I needed to do and I knew that if I needed anything else that I would need to ask for help. The reason, I imagine, is because you would experience the same at any job, if you're new at the job your co-workers and supervisor don't know your experience level so FastTrack pushing us to make ourselves a little uncomfortable now might lessen the sting of looking like a "noob" when we have to do it in the real world and I appreciate that.

When I'm not coding, or enjoying just sitting at home with my wife, daughter, and cat just doing nothing at all, I love spending time exploring some of the most beatiful sights Arizona has to offer. Otherwise I am always finding time to learn new ways to code and helping whoever I can.

Snack Overflow

A StackOverflow clone that is primarily oriented around food based questions and hot takes regarding food. Features full Create, Read, Update, Delete functionality for questions, answers, question likes, answer upvote/downvotes, a functional searchbar, and full user signup and login functionality.

Tiny Hub

Tiny Hub is a clone website of AirBnB, as my first fullstack application this site gives users the ability to create, read, update and delete spots for other users to stay at. It also gives users the ability to create, read and delete reviews for the spots so long as they are signed in, do not own the spot, and havent previously left a review.
